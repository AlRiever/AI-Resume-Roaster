02 Skills
38/100

A keyword dump. C, C++, Python, Java, JavaScript, HTML, CSS, MySQL, Git, MS Office — listed without evidence of depth in any of them. Recruiters at Google read this as "knows of these languages" rather than "can build with them."

Tip

Cut this list in half. Group what's left into "Proficient" (3–4 you can defend in an interview) and "Familiar." Drop MS Office entirely — it's noise on a SWE resume.

03 Projects
54/100

Three projects, all described in one line each. "Built an e-commerce site using HTML/CSS/JS" tells the reader nothing about complexity, scale, or your specific contribution. The chatbot project mentions Python but no library, no use case, no result.

Tip

Pick your strongest project and rewrite it as 3 bullets: (1) the problem, (2) the technical approach with specific tech, (3) a measurable outcome (users, performance, accuracy, anything quantifiable). Cut the other two if they don't survive the same treatment.

04 Internships
31/100

"1-month internship at ABC Tech (online). Worked on web development." This is the lowest-value sentence on the resume. It gives Google nothing to evaluate — what stack, what you owned, what shipped, what changed because you were there.

Tip

Rewrite as 2–3 bullets. Even from a 1-month remote internship: what feature did you build, what tech stack, what was the outcome. If you genuinely did nothing concrete, drop the internship — empty signal hurts more than a missing section.

05 Education
76/100

Standard format, CGPA visible (7.8 — solid but not eye-catching). Degree, institution, and dates all present. No relevant coursework listed, which is a missed chance.

Tip

Add a one-line "Relevant Coursework" sub-line: DSA, Operating Systems, Distributed Systems, Database Systems. This signals depth even before they read your projects.

06 Achievements
48/100

"Participated in college tech fest" is filler. "Solved 50+ problems on LeetCode" — Google recruiters don't care about volume; they care about depth and contest standing.

Tip

Replace LeetCode count with a contest rating (Codeforces, AtCoder, LeetCode contests) if you have one. If not, replace this section with a "Selected Projects" appendix or drop it entirely.

07 Hobbies
22/100

"Reading, Cricket, Watching movies." This section is dead weight in 2026. It tells Google nothing useful and consumes prime resume real estate.

Tip

Delete the section. Use the freed space for a stronger Projects section or a Summary line at the top.

What Google looks for

Google's entry-level bar emphasizes technical depth over breadth, demonstrable ownership of an end-to-end outcome, and signal density on the page. Their published rubrics weigh "complex projects with measurable impact" higher than a long skills list. This resume currently signals breadth without depth — exactly the opposite of what their hiring managers calibrate for.
